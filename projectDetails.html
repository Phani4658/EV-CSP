Project Information Details

In recent times, the need for sustainable transportation has become increasingly urgent. As concerns over climate change and environmental pollution intensify, the search for alternative modes of transportation that can mitigate these issues has gained significant traction. Electric vehicles have emerged as a promising solution, with their potential to reduce carbon emissions and dependence on non-renewable energy sources. However, the widespread adoption of Electric Vehicles as a daily mode of transportation faces numerous challenges, including public perceptions, infrastructure availability, and cost considerations.

The findings from this community survey project will contribute to the existing body of knowledge on electric vehicle acceptance and inform policy decisions aimed at promoting sustainable transportation options. Additionally, the survey results will provide insights to automotive manufacturers, infrastructure developers, and other stakeholders involved in the Electric Vehicle ecosystem, helping them tailor their strategies and initiatives to address the concerns and needs of potential electric vehicle users. Electric vehicles have emerged as a compelling solution to combat the environmental challenges posed by traditional internal combustion engine (ICE) vehicles. With the aim of reducing carbon emissions and dependency on fossil fuels, governments and communities worldwide have turned their attention to promoting electric vehicles as a daily mode of transportation. This community service project explores the evolution of electric vehicles globally, as well as their specific development in India and the state of Andhra Pradesh.

Evolution of Electric Vehicles Worldwide

The concept of electric vehicles dates back to the early 19th century when inventors like Thomas Davenport and Robert Anderson experimented with electric-powered transportation. However, it was not until the late 20th century that electric vehicles began to gain traction. The need for sustainable alternatives to gasoline-powered cars gained prominence due to concerns about climate change and the depletion of fossil fuel reserves.

Over the years, advancements in battery technology, improved charging infrastructure, and increased government support have propelled the growth of electric vehicles. Major automobile manufacturers have invested heavily in electric vehicle research and development, resulting in the production of highly efficient and affordable Electric Vehicle models. As a result, countries around the world have witnessed a significant surge in the adoption of electric vehicles, contributing to a greener and cleaner transportation ecosystem.

Evolution of Electric Vehicles in India

In India, the shift towards electric vehicles gained momentum with the introduction of the National Electric Mobility Mission Plan (NEMMP) in 2013. The NEMMP aimed to achieve national fuel security, reduce vehicular pollution, and promote sustainable growth in the automobile sector. Under this plan, various policy initiatives, financial incentives, and collaborations with industry stakeholders were implemented to encourage the adoption of electric vehicles.

The Indian government has also launched initiatives like the Faster Adoption and Manufacturing of Electric Vehicles in India (FAME India) scheme, which provides subsidies and incentives to manufacturers and consumers of electric vehicles. These measures, coupled with the growing awareness about environmental sustainability, have resulted in a gradual but steady increase in the number of electric vehicles on Indian roads.
